Homemade Pesto!

Introduction

Originally from the Italian city of Genoa, pesto has become a popular sauce worldwide. Although primarily used to dress pasta, pesto can be used with a variety of other foods, ranging from pizzas to salads. The word comes from the Genoese verb pestâ, meaning to grind or crush, as its ingredients were traditionally ground together using a mortar and pestle, although this recipe uses a blender. I chose pesto because of its versatility, and I've made pesto for many food-related school projects in the past. (It's also what I believe to be the best dressing to use on pasta!)

Original recipe :)

Ingredients

  • 3 cup fresh basil
  • ¾ cup grated Parmesan cheese
  • ½ cup olive oil
  • ¼cup pine nuts
  • 4 cloves garlic
  • 2 teaspoons lemon juice (optional)

Instructions

  1. Combine basil, Parmesan cheese, olive oil, pine nuts, and garlic in the bowl of a food processor or blender
  2. Blend to a smooth paste, adding lemon juice if desired
  3. Enjoy as a dip, on pasta, or in a sandwich!
